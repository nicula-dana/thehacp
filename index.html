Core Principles (HACP)

Hybrid Work as Default

Work is distributed across humans and autonomous agents, each contributing unique capabilities.

Roles as Functions, Not Titles

Responsibilities are dynamically claimed, not statically assigned.

Flow over Sprints

Work moves in responsive microloops triggered by real-world signals, not fixed time boxes.

Transparency by Design

Every decision or contribution is traceable and auditable, regardless of origin (human or AI).

Cognitive Load Awareness

Attention is treated as a finite asset, managed proactively via system-level protocols.

Trustworthy Autonomy

AI agents must explain their actions and meet trust thresholds for influence.

Recommended Role Tokens

Initiator (Human)

Proposes or signals new work based on emerging needs or insights.

Decider (Human)

Holds decision authority for specific loops or outcomes.

Synthesizer (Human)

Integrates input into coherent insight, strategy, or deliverable.

Validator (Human)

Confirms or rejects outcomes based on quality, relevance, or impact.

Narrator (Human)

Documents progress, lessons learned, and explains decisions.

Signal Scanner (Agent)

Continuously monitors data or events for loop-triggering signals.

Orchestrator (Agent)

Coordinates actions between humans and other agents.

Implementer (Agent)

Executes tasks, scripts, or routines autonomously.

Shadow Logger (Agent)

Records behind-the-scenes decisions and actions for auditability.

Explainer (Agent)

Renders AI reasoning transparent for human understanding.
